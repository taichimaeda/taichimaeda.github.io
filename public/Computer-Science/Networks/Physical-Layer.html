Physical Layer

Panels

  • Socket panel
    • Connects cables from offices.
  • Network switch
    • Creates LANs.
  • Private branch exchange
    • Interlinks phone systems.
    • Allows users to communicate via internal lines.

Coaxial Cables

Coaxial cables reduce the problems associated with UTP cables by placing the two conductors concentrically, which has considerable shielding benefits.

  • EM is mostly between inner and outer conductor.
  • Wider bandwidth.
  • Higher cost per meter.
    • UTP is commonly used for everyday uses.

TV cable systems still use coaxial cables.

Optical Fibres

Optical fibres do not suffer from EMI.

  • Exploits total refraction of light.
  • Attenuation is low
    • Can be used for long distances.
  • Supports very high frequencies.
  • 2-125 micrometers in diameter.

Wireless Transmission

In wireless transmission signals are carried in electromagnetic spectrum. It is intended for bidirectional communication and follows the inverse square law.

There are some propagation effects caused by environment:

  • Reflection
  • Obstruction by objects
  • Interference

Information Representation

Analogue data represents information in a continuous set of states, using a physical property of the channel, while digital represents information in a discrete set of states, using some coding scheme.

Baud rates and bit rates are components that determine the speed of communication:

  • Baud rate: number of changes in signal per second.
  • Bit rate: number of bits per second.

Modem and Codec

Digital channel can be implemented by an analogue channel using a modem. Conversely, analogue channel can be implemented by a digital channel using a codec.

Modems (digital to analog) and codecs (analog to digital) require:

  • Digital to Analogue Convertor (DAC)
  • Analogue to Digital Convertor (ADC)

Modulation Scheme

Modulation transforms a signal into a more suitable form for transmission.

  • Baseband modulation
    • No change in input signal.
  • Broadband modulation
    • Amplitude modulation
      • Higher amplitude represents 1.
      • Lower amplitude represents 0.
    • Phase modulation
      • Shift in phase indicates change in bit.
    • Quadratic phase modulation
    • N-quadrature amplitude modulation
      • Manipulates both amplitude and phase.

Digital Subscriber Line

DSL is a technique to make efficient use of phone lines for digital communication.

Phone lines artificially limited up to 3000 Hz bandwidth because normal voice frequencies range from 400 to 3400Hz. DSL removes this bandwidth filter for data transmission.

Below is a typical configuration of DSL channels with 1.1 MHz of bandwidth:

  • Divide bandwidth into 256 channels of 4000 Hz each.
  • Splitter separates voice band (0 - 4 kHz) from the rest.
  • ADSL modem performs modulation.
  • Channels 1–5 (4-25 kHz) not used to avoid interference with voice channel.
  • Allocate more channels for download than upload.
    • Internet users mostly download.
