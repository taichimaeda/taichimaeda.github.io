Simplified version:
paxos-simple.pdf (lamport.azurewebsites.net)

Assumptions:

  • Asynchronous, non-Byzantine model
  • Each process may act as a more than one agent
  • Each process has a persistent storage

Three classes of agents:

  • Proposers
    • Proposes proposals to acceptors
    • Each proposal has a value and unique number
      • Proposal number is not necessarily in temporal order
  • Acceptors
    • Accepts proposals proposed by the proposers
    • A proposal is chosen iff it is accepted by a strict majority of acceptors
    • An acceptor may accept more than one proposals
      • To avoid split vote situation where no proposal is accepted by a strict majority of acceptors
      • This implies that multiple proposals may be chosen
  • Learners
    • Learns proposals chosen by the acceptors
    • Acceptors notify learners about the chosen proposals

Requirements:

  • (P1) An acceptor must accept the first proposal that it receives
    • To be able to choose a value even if only one proposal is proposed by a single proposer
  • (P2) All chosen proposals must have the same value
    • To satisfy the safety property that at most a single value may be chosen

Now let us consider the variants of (P2) below:

  • (P2.a) For all chosen proposals with value , every higher-numbered chosen proposal has value
  • (P2.b) For all chosen proposals with value , every higher-numbered proposal accepted by any acceptor has value
  • (P2.c) For all chosen proposals with value , every higher-numbered proposal issued by any proposer has value
  • (P2.d) For all issued proposals with number and value , there exists a strict majority of acceptors , such that:
    • All proposals numbered less than accepted by an acceptor in have value .
  • (P2.e) For all issued proposals with number and value , there exists a strict majority of acceptors , such that either:
    • No proposal numbered less than has been accepted by an acceptor in .
    • The highest-numbered proposal that is numbered less than and accepted by an acceptor in has value .

(P2.a) (P2) because proposal numbers are totally ordered.
(P2.b) (P2.a) because every chosen proposal must be accepted by at least one acceptor.
(P2.c) (P2.b) because every accepted proposal must be proposed by at least one proposer.
(P2.d) (P2.c) because:

  • Assume (P2.d) holds.
  • For each chosen proposal with number :
    • Take any issued proposal with number and value , such that .
    • By (P2.d), there exists a strict majority of acceptors , such that:
      • All proposals numbered less than accepted by an acceptor in have value .
      • Since is a strict majority of acceptors, it must include at least one acceptor that accepted the chosen proposal .
    • Therefore must have value , and so (P2.c) holds.

(P2.e) (P2.d) because the invariant (P2.e) for the highest-numbered proposal ensures that it applies to all proposals numbered less than .

Therefore maintaining (P1) (P2.d) satisfies the requirements (P1) (P2).
