References

References - The Rustonomicon (rust-lang.org)

Rust’s references in a nutshell:

Quote

There are two kinds of reference:

  • Shared reference: &
  • Mutable reference: &mut

Which obey the following rules:

  • A reference cannot outlive its referent
  • A mutable reference cannot be aliased
  • A short lifetime cannot be coerced into a longer one.

Aliasing

Aliasing - The Rustonomicon (rust-lang.org)

Variables and pointers alias if they refer to overlapping regions of memory.

Lifetimes

Lifetimes - The Rustonomicon (rust-lang.org)

Lifetimes are named regions of code that a reference must be valid for. … Types which contain references (or pretend to) may also be tagged with lifetimes

In most of our examples, the lifetimes will coincide with scopes.

A reference (sometimes called a borrow) is alive from the place it is created to its last use.

Limits of Lifetimes

Limits of Lifetimes - The Rustonomicon (rust-lang.org)

Examples where the lifetime rules are respected but Rust is unable to understand it due to the limitation of its syntax/borrow checker:

  • Lifetime elision rules may relate two references inappropriately.
    • See example: Limits of Lifetimes - The Rustonomicon (rust-lang.org)
  • Lifetimes of the reference matched against in a match block conservatively extends to the end of the block.
    • See example: Limits of Lifetimes - The Rustonomicon (rust-lang.org)

Lifetime Elision

Lifetime Elision - The Rustonomicon (rust-lang.org)

Quote

Elision rules are as follows:

  • Each elided lifetime in input position becomes a distinct lifetime parameter.
  • If there is exactly one input lifetime position (elided or not), that lifetime is assigned to all elided output lifetimes.
  • If there are multiple input lifetime positions, but one of them is &self or &mut self, the lifetime of self is assigned to all elided output lifetimes.
  • Otherwise, it is an error to elide an output lifetime.

Most of the rules are straightforward, but check the examples just in case: Lifetime Elision - The Rustonomicon (rust-lang.org)

Subtyping and Variance

Subtyping and Variance - The Rustonomicon (rust-lang.org)

Rust does not have the notion of subtyping for normal types, only lifetimes.

Subtraits/supertraits are not part of subtyping - subtraits simply copy all the specs of their supertraits.
